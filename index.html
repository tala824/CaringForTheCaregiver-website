About Us

Structured, nursing-led, caregiver-inclusive support.

Caring for a loved one with Alzheimer's or dementia is one of the most complex experiences a family can face. The behavioral changes, the uncertainty, the weight of daily decisions — it requires more than good intentions. It requires expert guidance.

Caring for the Caregiver is a nursing-led health services practice focused on Alzheimer's disease and related neurocognitive conditions. Led by Dr. Caryn Blair, DNP, APRN, PMHNP-BC, we provide clinical assessments, caregiver-inclusive care planning, and ongoing supportive nursing services.

Our Services

Expert guidance for complex behavioral health needs.

Every care plan is built around your loved one's clinical needs, your needs as a caregiver, and the cultural context your family brings.

Thorough clinical evaluations conducted by DNP-prepared clinicians to identify behavioral and cognitive patterns — providing the clarity you need to move forward.

Our Approach

Clinical. Structured. Compassionate.

Our nursing-led approach combines clinical rigor with genuine understanding. Every engagement follows a structured process grounded in evidence-based practice.

01

Assess & Understand

We begin with a comprehensive psychiatric-mental health nursing assessment — evaluating your loved one's behavioral patterns and the impact on you as a caregiver.

02

Plan & Inform

Together, we develop a caregiver-inclusive care plan built on clear health information — translating clinical complexity into language that supports real decision-making.

03

Support & Adapt

Through ongoing supportive nursing services, we walk alongside you as conditions evolve — adjusting the care plan and ensuring consistent clinical guidance.

Common Questions

What to expect when you reach out.

We understand that seeking support is a significant step. Here are answers to questions we hear most often.

What types of services do you provide?

We provide three core nursing-led services: psychiatric-mental health nursing assessments, caregiver-inclusive care planning, and ongoing supportive nursing services. All services are delivered by DNP-prepared clinicians specializing in Alzheimer's and related neurocognitive conditions.

Is this counseling or therapy?

No. Caring for the Caregiver is a nursing-led health services practice — not a counseling or therapy practice. We provide clinical nursing assessments, health information, and structured care planning. We do not provide psychotherapy, medication management, or physician medical services.

How are consultations conducted?

We offer both in-person and virtual consultations to accommodate the demands of caregiving. We understand that your schedule often revolves around your loved one, and we work flexibly to meet your needs.

Do I need a referral to request a consultation?

No referral is required. Whether you are early in the caregiving journey or years into it, you can reach out directly. We will discuss your situation and determine how our services can best support you.

Is everything I share kept confidential?

Yes. All information shared during our consultations and services is held in strict confidence, in accordance with professional ethical standards and applicable privacy laws.
